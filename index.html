Made for practice, teaching and performance

We design our tools around everyday musical work: practicing along with recordings, preparing teaching materials, running rehearsals, building karaoke tracks or checking sounds before a show.

Instead of large, general-purpose audio software, each tool focuses on a single job and stays out of your way. You can combine them as needed: remove vocals, change key, slow things down and play parts on the piano — all in the same browser.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which browsers and devices do your tools support?

Our browser tools are published in the Chrome Web Store and are meant for modern Chromium-based browsers on desktop, such as Google Chrome and Microsoft Edge. For working with your own audio files on a computer, we offer separate Mac apps that you install from the App Store.

Do I need to upload my songs or videos to use your tools?

No. The extensions work directly with the websites and tabs open in your browser, and the Mac apps work with audio on your computer. The tools are designed for practical, real-world use without unnecessary steps, and all the processing happens in real time on your device.

Is there a free trial? How does pricing work?

The tools include a free trial period so you can see if they fit your workflow. After that, you can choose from subscription or one-time purchase options where available. Exact prices are always shown inside of the product before the checkout.

How can I manage or cancel my subscription?

Click the “Manage Subscription” button shown in the product and follow the instructions there.

Who should use these tools?

Our tools are designed for anyone working with music: singers, instrumentalists, choirs, bands, music schools, home-studio producers, DJs and content creators who use music in their videos or streams.
